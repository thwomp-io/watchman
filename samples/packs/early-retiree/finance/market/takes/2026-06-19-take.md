# Market take — 2026-06-19 close · the income book lags, by design

*A FICTIONAL sample take for the early-retiree persona — illustrative only, not advice.*

## The tape

A broad green day led by semis (SOXX/SMH) and megacaps. Risk-on, healthy breadth — a session built
for growth books, less so for a defensive, income-tilted one.

## The book — an income-tilted ~$2.9M retiree

The portfolio *underperformed* the tape today, and that is the entire point:

- **VTI** participated in the broad move, but it's a smaller slice here than in a growth book.
- **The dividend sleeve** — **SCHD, VYM** and the defensives (**JNJ, PG, KO**) — was flat-to-modestly-up;
  staples and pharma don't ride a semis day, and they're not supposed to.
- **BND** (a real ~20% bond sleeve) was flat — that's the cushion doing its job; it'll matter far more on
  the red days than it cost today.
- **JPM** added a little on the financials bid. The **cash runway** sat out, as intended.

## The takeaway

Lagging a risk-on tape is the *feature*, not a bug, for a portfolio whose job is to fund a retirement, not
to win a rally. Low beta, real ballast, a multi-year cash runway: the swings are small in both directions.
Income keeps arriving regardless of the tape. Stay the course — there's nothing here that a green semis day
should change.
